Full Court 1 on 1 Drill

Key techniques, spacing strategies, and tactical tips to improve individual skills in a full court 1 on 1 setting.

Helpful Tips

Most youth coaches agree that teaching man to man defense first is vital in building a foundation of defensive understanding, but man to man defense can mean many things. Full court man to man pressure, shell defense (help), and denial just to name a few. I believe understanding the differences is important in the development of young players. Full court one on one is a very important drill to do for athletes to understand on ball man to man defense.

Defensive Stance

  • Stay low and balanced (in an athletic stance) to react quickly to the ball handler.

    • Controlled Closeout

  • Closeout with small and choppy steps that allow you to remain an arm's length from your opponent.

    • Contain the Dribbler

  • Angle them to the sideline or help defense to close space. Use boundaries and teammates to your advantage. This is where your defensive slide effort comes in handy.

    • Understanding When You're Beat

  • When an opponent beats you with a first step or is about to gain an advantage of you, you must recognize and admit it.

    • Turn, Sprint and beat them to a spot

  • After you recognize that you are about to get beat, you must turn from a defensive stance into a sprinter's pose and sprint and beat your opponent to a spot to cut them off.

    • Do Not Move Like A Crab

  • The ball handler is running like it is a sprint. If they beat you, you cannot expect to keep up with them by moving laterally in a defensive slide position. You must turn and sprint.

    • Player Without A Ball Should Be Faster Than A Player With A Ball

  • Sprinting without a ball is much easier than with a ball. Defense should be able to get ahead.

    • Do Not Settle For Riding Someone's Hip

  • A lot of players settle for running side by side with an offensive player. We want to get between them and the basket. We want to become a barrier, not a shoulder buddy.
