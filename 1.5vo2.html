1.5 Mile VO2 Max / Cooper Test

VO2 Max is the maximum volume of oxygen an individual can utilize during intense exercise (McArdle, Katch, & Katch, 2021). It is a key indicator of cardiovascular endurance and aerobic capacity. Higher VO2 max allows athletes to sustain higher intensities longer, improving performance in endurance and team sports (ACSM, 2021).

1.5 Mile Cooper Test Description

The 1.5 Mile Cooper Test is a field test designed to estimate VO2 Max through running. Participants run 1.5 miles (2.4 km) as quickly as possible, and the total time is recorded. This test is easy to administer, requires minimal equipment, and provides a reliable estimate of aerobic fitness (Cooper, 1968; Noonan & Dean, 2000).

VO2 Max Formula

VO2 Max can be estimated using the following formula based on the 1.5 mile run time:

VO2 Max (ml/kg/min) = 483 ÷ Time (minutes) + 3.5
Example: An athlete completes 1.5 miles in 12 minutes:
VO2 Max = 483 ÷ 12 + 3.5 = 43.25 ml/kg/min
