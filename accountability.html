Accountability

Accountability is the bridge between values and behavior. It is how standards are reinforced, culture is protected, and trust is built within teams and organizations.

My Definition of Accountability

One of my favorite quotes is, "People externalize failure, but internalize success." I have no idea where I got this quote from, but it has always resonated with me. When I translate this into athletics, I mean people do not own up to failures. Across my athletic experiences, people tend to blame and point the finger when something does not go their way. It may be that the outcome of a game or match was a loss or a poor performance, but as soon as something negative occurs people tend to externalize it. I cannot count the amount of times where it was the coach's fault, the parent's fault, a teammates fault or a variety of other people or circumstances. Building a culture of accountability is so important these days. Athletes need to be aware that they are just as accountable, if not more, than any other party involved in a sport and the losses are a reflection of their actions as well. Secondly, when success is experienced, it should not only be owned by the athlete, but also by the village of supporters that the athlete had throughout the journey.
