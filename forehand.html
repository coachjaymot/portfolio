Key Forehand Fundamentals

A consistent forehand starts with preparation and ends with balance. These concepts apply across age groups and skill levels.

Grip & the Ready Position

  • The semi-western is by far the most common among ATP players. Prepare in an athletic stance with both hands on the racket, tilted in the up position.

    • Footwork

  • Master the Closed or Neutral Stance first. Focus on stepping in opposition: if right-handed, step forward with your left foot to turn side-to-target.

    • Set Position

  • Coil the body side-to-target into the "Star Position." This backswing preparation allows for explosive energy storage in the trunk.

    • Swinging Low to High

  • Develop consistency by swinging from the hip to the opposite shoulder. This creates the necessary arc for net clearance and safety.

    • Spacing and Contact Point

  • Strike the ball slightly out in front and to your dominant side. Use your off-arm to measure the ideal distance from your body.

    • Brush Up on the Ball

  • Use a "windshield wiper" motion to brush up on the ball. This generates TopSpin, keeping powerful shots inside the lines.
