Press & Press Break

When I first began coaching basketball, the teams that would dominate were the teams that would press. These teams would see if your team would break under pressure, typically followed by running up the score. After my teams and I endured some tough games, I decided that I never wanted my teams or myself to feel that bad ever again. The first tactical element of basketball that I teach now is a zone press and a zone press break. It is a horrible feeling for your team to struggle to get the ball past half court. But to learn how to beat it, one must teach it. The biggest emphasis with zone presses is always NO MIDDLE.

    • Press Break

    Although pressing is always a major issue for teams that have not prepared for it, you cannot practice a press break until you have taught the press. After players develop an understanding of the press, they can then begin to learn how to attack it. My most common press break against zone presses was the 1-4 as seen in the video below. I would have all players line up along the free throw line-extended. I would have the ball side wing drop down and get the ball from the inbounder. The ball-side elbow would flash out diagonally towards the sideline. Player positioned on the weakside elbow would flash towards the middle (I preferred diagonally). And the weak side player closest to the sideline would play cat and mouse, basically moving forward and backwards depending on what happened. When the ball was inbounded and everyone would cut to their spots, I would always tell the person with the ball to remember S.M.B. This stood for sideline, middle, and back. This acronym would guide how they would scan the floor for teammates entering the gaps. As soon as the ball would touch the middle, the press was often in shambles.
