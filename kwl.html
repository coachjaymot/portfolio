KWL Charts

Learn how KWL charts can help athletes track knowledge, set goals, and reflect on what they have learned.

What is a KWL Chart?

  • KWL stands for what do studentsKnow, Want to Know, and what have they Learned.
  • It organizes learning into three simple categories.
  • Originally used in classrooms to track student learning and understanding.

    • Pre-Training: Know (K)

  • Athletes list what they already know about a skill or tactic. This is incredibly useful for a coach or teacher as it shares what foundational knowledge an athlete already possesses and makes it transparent to the coach or educator.
  • Example: “I know the correct grip for a forehand.”
  • Activates prior knowledge and sets the foundation for learning.

    • Goal-Setting: Want to Know (W)

  • Athletes identify what they want to learn or improve.
  • Example: “I want to know how to increase serve power.”
  • Encourages curiosity and directs practice focus.

    • Reflection: Learned (L)

  • Athletes reflect on what they learned after practice or feedback.
  • Example: “I learned adjusting my stance improved my serve accuracy.”
  • Supports retention and understanding of progress.

    • Why Use KWL in Sports?

  • Connects new skills to what athletes already know. Provides coaches with a baseline to scaffold off of.
  • Helps set clear goals for improvement.
  • Encourages reflection to strengthen performance over time.
  • Improves both individual and team learning experiences.

    • Practical Applications

  • Pre-game: plan strategies and review prior knowledge.
  • Skill practice: track what athletes know, want to know, and learn.
  • Coaching sessions: tailor instruction based on athlete responses.
  • Team debrief: reflect on what worked, what didn’t, and next steps.
