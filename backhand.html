Tennis Backhand

A dependable backhand is built on balance, timing, and efficient movement.

Key Backhand Fundamentals

Whether one-handed or two-handed, strong backhands rely on preparation, spacing, and clean contact.

Grip & Preparation

  • Two main variations: two-handed and one-handed. On a two-handed backhand, ensure your non-dominant hand is the dominant, power-producing hand.

    • Recognition & Footwork

  • Mirror your forehand movements. Turn side-to-target quickly. Recognize the ball early to make coiling and stepping second nature.

    • Set Position

  • The side-to-target preparation position. Keep the racket cocked back and ready to move forward through the ball.

    • Swinging Low to High

  • Mirror the forehand arc. Swing from the hip to the opposite shoulder to ensure consistent lift and safety over the net.

    • Contact Point

  • Strike the ball slightly out in front of the body. This allows both eyes to track the ball and ensures the body supports the racket.

    • Follow-Through & Recovery

  • Allow the swing to continue upward and over the shoulder. Finish the motion to naturally decelerate the racket head.
