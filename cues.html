The Importance of Cues

Understanding cues can dramatically improve learning, performance, and communication in sports and coaching.

What is Academic Vocabulary?

The inclusion of academic vocabulary is one of many elements that cause academic subjects to be respected. To demonstrate learning and comprehension in an academic area, one must understand the subject specific terminology or academic vocabulary in that area. The question that many coaches and physical educators struggle to answer is, "What represents our academic vocabulary?" The answer to that is movement cues! Cues are our academic language. When we teach movement skills and techniques we need to be mindful of breaking movements into cues. Cues are a great way to comprehend movements. They also are great for athletes to refer back to when they are not performing a skill properly. See some samples below.

Basketball Shooting - B.E.E.F.

Balance (feet shoulder width apart with knees bent)

Eyes on the target

Elbow (flexion at the elbow)

Follow through (hand should finish like it is in a coookie jar)

Tennis Forehand

Ready position - Feet shoulder width apart, knees slight bent

Side to target/unit turn

Swing low to high of from your hip to your opposite shoulder

Follow through
