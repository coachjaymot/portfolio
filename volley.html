Tennis Volley

Quick reactions and compact swings are essential for effective net play in tennis.

Key Volley Fundamentals

Mastering the volley requires early preparation, balanced footwork, and controlled contact.

Racquet Up

  • When we are located near the net or we are likely going to receive a volley it is important to have your racket up. It takes more time to bring your racket up if you are resting it with a limp arm than if you are in ready position with your racket up already. Keeping your racket up keeps the volley movement compact as opposed to making it a much bigger motion if you have your racket down.

    • Continental Grip & make a V

  • Other strokes tend to have grip variations, but continental is the go-to grip for volleys. I also tell beginners to make a V between their forearm and the racket.

    • Split Step

  • Split your feet as your opponent strikes the ball and land softly on both. Then prepare to turn your body (starting with your feet) to volley.

    • Step Towards the Ball

  • After we split step and begin to turn our body towards the ball, we need to step towards the ball. This helps transfer our weight forward.

    • Short Punch & Contact In Front

  • Use a compact punch. No back swing and no follow through. Meet the ball in front of your lead hip.

    • Recover

  • Since you are at the net you will have less time to recover and react to the next ball. It is very important that after you volley a ball that you prepare as if a ball will be coming right back quickly.
