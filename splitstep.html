Split Step

A well-timed split step places an athlete in an athletic position, allowing for quicker and more efficient movement in any direction. What really is the split step though? Well a split step is quick jump or hop that allows us to land on both feet simultaneously. Essentially, it is a two-footed landing from a hop. Now many people simply correlate it with getting ready to strike a ball, but I like to go a step further with my players. In tennis, we often do not know where our opponent is going to hit the ball until they strike the ball and we make the read. As our opponent hits the ball, we should be split stepping and then as we recognize where this ball is going we are going to move towards the ball using that split step hop to our advantage. By having a two-foot landing we can move in either direction effectively without losing time. If a ball comes close to our body we can use the split step to our advantage by pivoting on whichever foot will allow us to get into the closed-stance and hit a solid ground stroke.
