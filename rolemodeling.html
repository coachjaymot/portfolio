Role Modeling

Role modeling is leadership in action. Long before athletes hear what is expected, they observe what is accepted.

What Role Modeling Really Is

Role modeling is the practice of demonstrating acceptable behaviors. As leaders, we have the ability to set the tone, but there are multiple ways in which we can use role modeling to our benefit. When coaching teams, one of the first things that I would do is pull aside the veteran athletes. I would explain to them how important it is during tryouts and during the first weeks to behave a certain way because the younger athletes are always watching. If veteran players loaf, talk back to the coaches, or act inappropriately, this is what the younger athletes will find appropriate in that setting, so I always made it a point to do this and it can truly change a program around as it sets the tone for what the norms are and if those norms are productive and positive, you will likely experience more success.
